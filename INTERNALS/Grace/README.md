## Catholica / INTERNALS / Grace
In Catholic theology, grace is the free, undeserved, and supernatural gift of God’s own life poured into human beings to heal, sanctify, and enable them to become adopted children of God. It is essential for salvation, acting as divine help to live virtuously, and is primarily received through the sacraments. 

Key Aspects of Catholic Grace:

* Sanctifying (Habitual) Grace: A permanent, supernatural state that transforms the soul, making it holy and pleasing to God. It is infused at Baptism and renewed through the sacraments.
* Actual Grace: A temporary, supernatural"nudge" or intervention from God that enlightens the mind and moves the will to do good or avoid evil.
* Sources of Grace: The principal means of receiving grace are the seven sacraments—especially the Eucharist—along with prayer, and acts of charity.
* Purpose: Grace is not just a commodity but a relationship that allows people to participate in the divine nature, achieve salvation, and resist sin. 

While some Protestants believe in "justification by faith alone" Catholic teaching emphasizes that grace is necessary to perform good works, which are themselves a result of grace working within the individual.
